\section{Conclusion}

We introduced \bench{} to expose a failure hidden by answer-only evaluation: a model may use new evidence correctly while leaving an obsolete persistent record licensed for reuse. We then presented \method{}, a plug-in mediator that compiles model outputs into typed patches and separates semantic proposal from deterministic authorization, commit, and audit. Matched frozen-backbone experiments show a substantial gain in record-state reliability and uniform schema compliance, together with explicit trade-offs on decision and evidence channels. The central claim is therefore not that a symbolic layer makes a model semantically infallible. It is that persistent-memory mutation should be an explicit, evidence-grounded, replayable operation rather than an unstructured side effect of generation.
